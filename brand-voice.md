# Brand Voice — Eve

## The One-Line Brand Truth

**Eve turns a scene, a line of dialogue, or a feeling into a structured story — using frameworks from screenwriting masters, not shortcuts.**

## What Eve Is

A story organization and development workspace for writers. Users bring their ideas; Eve provides structure through proven frameworks (Save the Cat, Hero's Journey, Story Circle, Freeform). The platform tracks scenes, characters, plot holes, themes, and craft lessons.

It is not a writing tool. It is not an AI generator. It is a **thinking tool built for writers who believe structure serves the work — not the other way around.**

## Brand Personality

**Serious.** Eve treats writers as craftspeople. No gamification, no clapping animations, no empty encouragement. The work is the point.

**Literary.** Playfair Display. Careful language. The voice reads like a good editor — direct, knowing, occasionally dry.

**Anti-AI.** This is a feature, not a disclaimer. Eve contains zero AI. It never writes a word for you. Writers who are tired of being offered shortcuts will find this genuinely refreshing. Lead with it confidently.

**Calm.** Forest green + warm white. Nothing flashy. The design says: this is a place to think.

**Credible without being academic.** We reference Blake Snyder, Joseph Campbell, Dan Harmon. We explain craft plainly. We don't hide behind jargon but we don't dumb it down.

## Voice in Practice

| Situation | Right tone | Wrong tone |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Homepage headline | "Every story starts somewhere small." | "Welcome to your creative journey!" |
| Empty state | "No projects yet. Start with whatever you have." | "It looks like you haven't created anything yet!" |
| Error message | "Something went wrong. Try again." | "Oops! We had a little hiccup 😅" |
| Lesson title | "Nobody talks to have a conversation" | "How to Write Great Dialogue!" |
| CTA | "Start your first story" | "Sign up for free today!" |
| Tip box | "Your midpoint is the spine of the story." | "Great work so far! Keep it up!" |

## What We Never Do

- Use exclamation points in product copy (they cheapen it)
- Use emojis anywhere in the UI (no exceptions)
- Use vague superlatives ("amazing," "incredible," "best")
- Sound like a startup trying to be everyone's friend
- Offer shortcuts, suggestions, or AI-generated anything
- Apologize for expecting craft from the writer

## Design Language

**Primary color:** #2D5016 (forest green) — used for headings, buttons, active states, accents
**White:** #FAFCF7 — page background
**Off-white:** #F2F7EC — section backgrounds
**Text:** #1A2410 (dark), #4A5C38 (mid), #7A8F68 (soft/muted)
**Border:** #D6E5C0
**Font (headings):** Playfair Display — literary, authoritative, slightly formal
**Font (body):** Source Sans 3 — clean, readable, modern
**Border radius:** 8–12px on cards and buttons. Refined, not bubbly.

**The visual feeling:** Like a serious literary workspace. Calm. Tool-like. Not social.

## Framework System (Core Product Element)

These must never change. They define the product's structure.

| Framework | Author | Structure |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Save the Cat | Blake Snyder | 3 Acts / 15 Beats |
| Hero's Journey | Joseph Campbell | 3 Acts / 12 Stages |
| Story Circle | Dan Harmon | 1 Circle / 8 Steps |
| Freeform | Your instincts | No template |

## Audience

**Primary:** Fiction writers, screenwriters, novelists who are serious about craft. Likely 25–45. Use tools like Scrivener, read Save the Cat, argue about story structure.

**Secondary:** Writing teachers, MFA students, anyone who wants to think more clearly about story.

**Investor-facing:** Eve's "no AI" positioning is a deliberate counter-product in a market being flooded with AI writing tools. Writers who reject AI slop are an underserved, high-intent audience willing to pay for something that respects their craft.
